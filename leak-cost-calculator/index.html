Frequently Asked Questions -- City of Smyrna Water and Sewer Rates

How much does a dripping faucet cost per year in Smyrna GA?

A faucet dripping at 60 drops per minute wastes about 91 gallons per month. At the combined baseline marginal rate of $14.38 per 1,000 gallons, that adds approximately $1.30 per month or $16 per year.

Does a leak raise my Smyrna sewer bill?

Yes. The City of Smyrna bills sewer at a flat $9.30 per 1,000 gallons with no cap or winter-average mechanism. Every gallon lost to an indoor leak adds to the sewer bill in addition to the water bill.

What are the City of Smyrna water rates?

Effective January 1, 2026: base charge $15.18/month. Tiers per 1,000 gal: T1 (0-3k) $5.08, T2 (3-7k) $6.76, T3 (7-14k) $8.42, T4 (14k+) $9.85. Sewer: $9.30/1,000 gal flat.

Can I get a Smyrna leak adjustment on my bill?

Yes. Once the leak is repaired, you may qualify for a Smyrna Leak Adjustment on the sewer portion of your bill. Form at smyrnaga.gov.
